What is Mental Health?

Mental health is all about how we feel, think, and connect with the world around us. It shapes how we handle stress, relate to people, and make everyday choices. It’s not just about avoiding illness — it’s about feeling balanced, supported, and able to cope when life gets tough.

What Mental Health Covers:

  • Emotional wellbeing: Being able to feel your feelings — sadness, joy, anger — and manage them in healthy ways.
  • Resilience: Bouncing back after hard times and finding strength even when things feel heavy.
  • Social connection: Having people around you who get you, support you, and help you feel less alone.
  • Clear thinking: Making decisions that feel right and staying grounded when your mind feels busy.

Why Mental Health Matters:

Mental health isn’t just important — it’s essential.

It affects:

  • Every stage of life, from growing up to growing older.
  • Your physical health — sleep, energy, even your immune system.
  • Your relationships, your work, and how you feel day to day.

Some Common Mental Health Struggles:

You might have heard of these — or felt some of them yourself:

  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • Bipolar Disorder
  • Schizophrenia
  • Post-traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Eating Disorders

Whatever it is, you are not broken. You are human. And you are not alone.

How to Take Care of Your Mental Health:

  • Get enough sleep, eat well, and move your body
  • Stay connected to people who lift you up
  • Find ways to manage stress — even small ones help
  • Reach out for help when you need it — therapy, medication, or just a chat with someone who listens
If You’re Struggling Right Now: Please do not ignore it. You matter. You are not alone and help is available.
