What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to a person’s emotional, psychological, and social wellbeing. It affects how people think, feel, and behave, as well as how they handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Key Aspects of Mental Health:

  • Emotional wellbeing: Managing emotions like sadness, anger, and happiness.
  • Psychological resilience: Coping with stress and recovering from setbacks.
  • Social functioning: Building healthy relationships and staying connected.
  • Cognitive functioning: Thinking clearly and making sound decisions.

Why Mental Health Matters

  • Important at every life stage — childhood to adulthood.
  • Influences physical health (e.g., sleep, immunity, heart).
  • Affects productivity, quality of life, and relationships.

Common Mental Health Conditions:

  • Depression
  • Anxiety Disorders
  • Bipolar Disorder
  • Schizophrenia
  • Post-traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Eating Disorders

Mental Health Maintenance Includes:

  • Regular sleep, nutrition, and exercise
  • Social connections and support
  • Managing stress
  • Seeking professional help (e.g., therapy, medication)
If you feel like you might have a mental health condition: Take it seriously. You’re not alone — help is available.
